Introduction

Yiwu Yao is a medicinal chemist, drug discoverer who is experienced in developing novel small molecules target disease-causing protein, especially epigenetic regulators. He is also a skillful organic chemist experienced in the total synthesis of complex natural products. He currently works as a Research Investigator at the University of Michigan in Tomasz Cierpicki and Jolanta Grembecka lab. Our lab was the first to discover menin-KTM2A inhibitors for AML treatment, which led to a recently FDA-approved drug Revumenib.

    Professional Profile
  • Advanced a first-in-class project from hit to pre-clinical study.
  • Productive researcher with strong publication record in top-tier journals such as Nature Chemical Biology and the Journal of Medicinal Chemistry.
  • Excellent communication skills to interact with researchers specialized in structural, chemical, cell, and animal studies.

Research Interests

  • Development of small molecules to inhibit epigenetic proteins function in cancer cells.
  • Developing small molecules targeting PRC1 core subunit Ring1A/B.
  • Identification and development of small molecule HDACs inhibitors.
  • Total synthesis of bioactive natural products.
